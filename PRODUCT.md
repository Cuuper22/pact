# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Groups who are physically together and feel the pull anyway: friends at dinner, couples on a date, families at the weekend table, study groups. One motivated host starts it; everyone else joins with a single scan and zero patience for onboarding. Context of use: evening, low light, mid conversation, phone face down. The job: stay present with the people in the room without relying on willpower alone.

## Product Purpose

Lock every phone at the table into one shared session, with no accounts, across iOS and Android. Unlocking for one person requires the consent of everyone present; leaving is always possible and always visible. Success is measured in repeat tables (pacts per crew per week, median pact length, recap shares), not time in app. Less time in app is the point.

## Brand Personality

Considered, warm, unbothered. Adult and direct, never preachy about phones, never cute about discipline. The app should feel like it wants to be put down: quiet surfaces, one ember of warmth, copy that talks like a friend who tells the truth.

## Anti-references

- Forest's gamified cuteness and mascot guilt. Pact is for adults at a table, not a tamagotchi.
- Duolingo-style streak shame and pestering notifications. Pact never messages you outside a live session.
- Parental-control jail aesthetics (Bark, Qustodio): surveillance vibes are fatal for a consent product.
- SaaS landing slop: gradient text, glassmorphism, hero metrics, neon dopamine UI.

## Design Principles

1. Glass, not steel: never prevent leaving, make leaving visible instead.
2. The table is the user: every in-session screen is designed to be read by the group, not the owner.
3. Friction with manners: asking is one tap, granting is one tap, and the cheap glance is the only thing that gets expensive.
4. Dark at dinner: during a pact, light is pollution. The screen earns its lumens.
5. Nothing to log into: ephemerality is the privacy model, not a policy page.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA minimum. Large tap targets for allow and not-now (used in low light, mid conversation). Never color alone for vote states; pair icon plus label. Reduced-motion alternative for the flame (static ember, crossfade). VoiceOver and TalkBack labels on the shield and night screens. Emergency calling and SOS are never impeded on either platform, by architecture and by policy.
